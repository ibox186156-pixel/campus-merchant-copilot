# Campus Merchant Copilot - Detailed Opportunity Validation

## 1. Opportunity Judgement

**Target users.** The first beachhead is university-area micro merchants: milk tea shops, coffee carts, light-meal stores, print shops, stationery shops, parcel stations, and student-service kiosks with 1-5 staff and limited financial management capacity.

**Pain points.**

- They operate with thin cash buffers and make inventory decisions by intuition.
- Payment and delivery data exist, but the owner rarely turns them into daily decisions.
- Marketing copy is improvised, and discounts often hurt margin.
- Financing applications are hard because receipts, supplier invoices, and cash-flow explanations are fragmented.
- Owners are time-poor: bookkeeping happens late at night after the shop closes.

**Why now.**

- Payment, delivery, and review data are already digital enough for a lightweight workflow.
- OCR and document understanding can parse receipts, tables, and supplier documents.
- Financial reasoning models can turn raw data into explainable, compliance-aware recommendations.
- Duxiaoman's recent AI direction around financial LLMs, reasoning, and OCR creates a credible technical and strategic fit.

## 2. User Evidence

**Survey evidence.** AP's report on a Slack survey of 2,000 small business owners found that about 40% saw 2024 as a critical year, 43% were exploring productivity technology, about half were expanding marketing, and 45% were saving emergency funds. This supports the need for low-cost efficiency and cash-flow tooling.

**Academic evidence.** A 2025 SME financial management deployment paper highlights that small firms have limited data and need accounts receivable and cash-flow forecasting tools designed for operational constraints. A 2025 SME transaction categorisation paper points to messy transaction descriptions, sparse labels, and information asymmetry as barriers to cash-flow lending.

**Competitive signal.** Intuit launched AI agents for QuickBooks in 2025, showing that AI operating agents for small business finance are becoming a mainstream category. The gap in China is a localized, mobile-first, receipt-aware copilot for micro merchants rather than accounting teams.

**Desk research interpretation.** The pain is not only "record my books." The stronger wedge is "tell me what to do today and make my business more finance-readable."

## 3. MVP Sample

**Prototype.** The clickable prototype in `index.html` demonstrates:

- Cash-flow risk dashboard.
- AI daily brief with inventory, marketing, and financing recommendations.
- Receipt-scan simulation.
- Prompt flow for concierge testing.
- 14-day validation plan.

**AI workflow.**

1. Ingest: payment export, receipt images, delivery orders, stock notes, reviews, weather, and school calendar.
2. Normalize: OCR, category mapping, supplier matching, anomaly flags, margin estimation.
3. Reason: forecast working capital, detect waste, suggest promotions, score finance readiness.
4. Act: generate merchant tasks, group-chat copy, purchase list, and credit-file memo.

**Core prompt.** The prompt forces the AI to output only three actions and include cost, expected impact, confidence, and downside risk. It explicitly prohibits promising credit approval.

**Trust details that matter.**

- Every recommendation includes the data evidence used.
- The product flags missing or low-quality data instead of hallucinating.
- Financing output is framed as a preparation memo, not a loan decision.
- Merchant consent and data minimization are designed into onboarding.

## 4. Commercial Judgement

**Paying users.**

- Individual shop owners pay for time savings and better operating decisions.
- Campus food courts and mini-chain operators pay for multi-shop visibility.
- Financial institutions pay for lower-cost, explainable merchant pre-underwriting signals.
- SaaS/POS/channel partners pay for retention and value-added services.

**Pricing.**

- Starter: ¥29/month for receipt parsing and daily brief.
- Pro: ¥99/month for cash-flow forecast, inventory alerts, promotion generation, and finance-readiness memo.
- Institution/API: ¥6-12 per active merchant per month, plus setup fee for data integration.

**Acquisition.**

- Start with one university business street and recruit 20 shops through in-person visits.
- Partner with cashier SaaS, printer consumable sellers, food court managers, and campus group-buying operators.
- Publish a weekly campus merchant pulse report to create merchant referrals.

## 5. Two-Week Validation Plan

**Days 1-3: problem discovery.** Interview 15 merchants. Collect 30 anonymized receipts or daily summaries. Test whether owners accept data authorization and whether ¥29/month feels reasonable.

**Days 4-6: concierge MVP.** Use OCR, spreadsheet normalization, and LLM prompting to generate daily briefs manually behind a polished front-end prototype.

**Days 7-10: live pilot.** Deliver daily briefs to five shops. Track open rate, action execution, time saved, cash-flow prediction error, and trust objections.

**Days 11-14: commercial proof.** Ask for prepayment or signed pilot intent. If at least two shops prepay and one channel partner agrees to data access, continue to a six-week private beta. If not, pivot the wedge to inventory alerts or promotion copywriting.

## Extra Details an Examiner Should Notice

- **Compliance boundary:** The MVP never claims lending approval; it prepares evidence and explains risk.
- **Data strategy:** Start with merchant-owned data and add integrations only after willingness-to-pay is proven.
- **Failure modes:** Poor OCR, incomplete ledgers, fake receipts, seasonal school calendar effects, and owner distrust.
- **Defensibility:** Local merchant workflow data, finance-specific prompt/evaluation set, and channel partnerships.
- **Kill criteria:** Below 30% action execution, no willingness to authorize data, or no paid pilot after two weeks.
